(No Spoilers)
I've seen some awesome levels in my day but wow! This tells one of the most inspiring stories of all time, in any piece of media.

The first screen represents frustration. Anyone who has played a Mario game will tell you that the most difficult moments are the underwater stages and the Final Bosses. The Volcanic Debris is also a memory of World 8, one of the most toughest worlds in the game. This represents bad memories. The flickering fence represnts vison. Mario is so caring for Peach that he would go blind to save her.

And then, the Bowser Fight.
This is one of the best battles in gaming history. The green Koopa represnts luck, you know you can defeat Bowser once and for all. The roar that comes out of Bowser's Mouth represents trouble. You know stuff has gone wrong and you need to stop it. We jump on the ! switch and have Bowser fall into the lava underwater. This represents... I don't know. Then we see one of the best plot twists in all of anime. Kamek was Peach the whole time! And we learn that he's 100% gay. He (she?) then uses her (his?) magic wand to grow Bowser into a sexy beast that can breathe huge flames! Oh boy, this represents... There is a word for it at least.

The beanstalk represents ascending the heavens. Mario is basically Jesus and Bowser is the roman soldier trying to pin him down on the cross. Considering that today is Easter, it makes a ton of sense. The thwomps represents the crosses. I could remember there being 3 crosses but whatever, I'm sure you forgotten, I'll forgive you. The Yoshi represents extinction. It is confirmed that Mario killed all the dinosours. The multiple Larrys represent fear. Larry is scared because you are a horrible monster and decided to kill him for no good reason.

And then, the ending. The ending is simply beautiful. The slow motion makes the scene intense and awesome. Who needs a good framerate? Losers, that's who! The falling rocks are also a nice touch and gives us more proof that Mario is a monster. The fish in the background represnt depression. Everyone is mournful that Bowser died. Peach falling from the heavens proves that Mario was really on mushrooms the whole time

So yeah, that was an awesome level! It didn't have that many glitches and I do like the custom tileset you made. I will give a score as you see below:

 Super Mario Green Star Groove DEMO Review

So I remember finding Horizon a couple of days before making an account and one of the first hacks I saw was this one. It looked promising and I even made a post talking about my feelings. Nice of Cloudy Luigi to invite me to his picnic. This hack contains a bunch of levels which include a fortress, a red Toad house, and a green Toad House. Let's a hope right in!

The first thing I want to mention is the nice remix of the NSMBDS Title Screen Music on the file selection screen. I find it to be pretty nice. The plot is a little generic but I guess since it is a demo, what do I expect? The fadeouts are cool too, would like to know which file it is to change. I want to change the Bowser fadeout in particular. I also like how in each world, the flagpoles look different. I like that. Now, onto the levels.

D-1 - Fire Flower Hill:
This level is pretty nice for a 1-1. There isn't that much say other than one flaw. When you spin on the flowers, they don't spit out coins. But other than that, it is pretty good.

D-2 - Koopa Troopa Cranny:
A pretty decent level as well. I also can't really say much about the level other than it is a pretty good cavern based level. I do feel like the 1st Star Coin is tedious though. It takes like a minute to find one. I also find the 2nd Star Coin to be too easy. I do like the Larry boss fight as it reminds me of the boss fights in Sonic 2 where the second level contains a boss. I do think finding the 3rd Star Coin is cryptic but the way you got it was totally unexpected! I like that.

D-3 - Propeller Heights:
A nice athletic level. It reminds of some levels in NSMB2 like the one in World 1 or a bunch in World 5. I do like the bonus level as you just kill koopas because you are a horrible monster. And I do like the Luigi Easter Egg
D-Fortress - Hammer Bros Hideout.

Oh boy, how do I describe it? You start out outside the fortress which I like and you could even leave it. But once you enter it, I have bad war memories. Firstly, I find defeating the Hammer Bros. frustrating. They are trapped in block that you can't go up so you have to be crowded to defeat them. Worse, the 2nd Star Coin is easier! I constantly go back to a Toad House to get more power-ups. The remix is quite nice though.

D-4 Urchins Underwater:
Another great level. It takes place underwater and the level's main enemy is the urchins. I think it is a nice level and I even like the sunken airship. I wonder if that's is a reference to the sunken airship from SMW? I do find the bubbles for the 2nd Star Coin to be annoying but overall, a nice level.

D-5 Schroced Steppes:
*INSERT POSTIVE COMMENT HERE* This level uses the sand spouts and it isn't a bad gimmick. I do think the ? blocks look weird though and getting the 3rd Star Coin is difficult

D-6 River Piranha Oasis:
I like this theme. It takes place in a grassy oasis. The main mechanic is the River Piranha Plants and the level takes great advantage of that mechanic. I do have one complaint. It isn't clear if the slopes are solid. I do feel like you could made them a bit lighter.

D-7 - Doki Doki Dunes:
Oh boy! Firstly, we need more Doki Doki Panic references! This level starts off with the player in a cave with a door. I don't know if that is reference to the plot of SMB2 but if it is, I like it. This level is full SMB2 references with the Shy Guys and the Green Pokeys. I like how the subspace areas are done. More official Mario games should do that. I do feel like getting the second star coin is a little tricky due to the bob-omb being difficult to place. Another cool things are the vases. Entering them causes a Flame-Chomp to appear, similar to the masks in SMB2. I think the Angry Sun or the Mad Moon would have made more sense but hey, whatever, I like it. I really got to wonder what the point of cherries were. I collected all of them and yet nothing happened. I find the Roy boss fight to be alright but the Huckit Crab were kind of annoying.


D-8 Cooligan's Chill Hill:
A pretty boring level. I find getting the 2nd Star Coin impossible and at one point I had to use a switch but I didn't know what it did. The 2nd half of the level is boring and empty with just cooligans. I feel like adding something like Snow Spikes could have been more unique and special.

D-10 - Rise of the Cheeps:
Oh lord... This is the 2nd worst level in the hack. The first star coin is a pain to get to if you went from the midway and Cheep Cheeps were annoying as they kept jumping at you. I thought that was the worst level in the hack until...

D-11 - Toxic Creek Leak:
This is by far the worst level in the hack. The bramballs are demonic spiders and having to kill one of them in the level to change blocks was stupid! Each bramball always had their legs squash me. I do like the level theme but that isn't saying much.

D-12 - Lemmy's Chaos Cavaravan:
And the 3rd worst level in the hack. I find the music pretty bad and the 1st Star Coin is hard as autoscrolling and throwing bombs does not equal fun. Things just come at you and adding a spike to Lemmy's boss made it more frustrating. I honestly rage quitted at the Lemmy fight. There are hardly any positives unless you count that cute little banner for some tires or oil or something later on.

D-Green Star Houes - Green Star House A:
A fun challenge and a breath of fresh air from those 3 terrible levels. Reminds me of the Sprixie House from SM3DW. My favorite was the one with the Stone Spikes.


D-Special - Walking the Plains (NSMBDS 1-1):
Oh boy, an amazing remake! It reminds of the coin battle version of SMB1 1-1 as some things are remixed. I liked the underground section, especially the logs. There isn't a whole lot else to be said.

So overall, I'll give this hack a Good/Good (Bad, Average, Good). Sure, I may have been negative in some points but there are mostly good to make me not hate this hack. I also didn't explain very much on what the hack does right as it is pretty difficult to say why. Saying what it does wrong though is a little easier IMO.

I am looking very forward to this hack and I already consider it my second favorite NSMBW hack next to NewerSMBW. Good luck, Cloudy Luigi.
