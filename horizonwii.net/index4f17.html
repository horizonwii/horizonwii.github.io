 I played through the entirety of the hack this afternoon, and I quite liked it!
some stuff:
Positive Remarks:
-I like the difficulty (for the most part) Newer never rly did get quite that difficult, but this hack had me dying alot (which is a good thing overal)
-I also like the theming; every stage feels very unique and varied, with the consistant theme of spookiness not getting in the way of having different stage-types
-the music was a good choice (gotta love dkc music ) although the final stage's music was maybe a bit too much

Improvable stuff:
-the maze stage (1-5) feelt more like a SMM stage then a real NSMBWII stage, it was confusing (not in the right way) and some areas seemed there just to make the player get more lost, I feel like making it a bit more linear with maybe a better indication of where to go would have been a good idea (also placing the checkpoint not right before the redicoulously easy bossfight but in the middle of the stage woulda made more sense)
-In general, some stages (1-7 in particular) feelt to long; without introducing new concepts or varying things up, had it not been for the punnishing and hard level design, most stages quickly get borring
-the final stage: I like the idea of making the final stage a hard test of your platforming skills, in a way it feelt more like a super meatboy stage then a NSMB stage. Then again, the way this is done is by using annoying and sometimes straight up infuriating stage hazards and obstacles; I like the firebars, they make for fun avoiding-platforming, the rolling hills goomba and fire bros combo was interesting, but the varying speeds of the goombas due to the hills and the fireball spam made this section very infuriating. the red block section was cool (though not multi-player-friendly in the slightest) although it feelt more aimed towards the player beeing small since you were forced to make ducked-jumps a few times in order to keep your powerup. The spike section was cool at first, but the second and especially third bom-blasting section was hard, which the constant firing of both fireballs as well as magic spells didnt help with. using the spin to-move platforms combined with spike-pillars as the final hazard was a bit annoying, since it once again took advantage of weird combinations of level hazards. Now for the final boss: before entering I assumed this to be an easy boss, given that the original Big Boo boss in newer was very easy, but once I realised that the wind was a factor, I almost lost both my sanity, as well as the bossfight. The only way I ended up beating it was because I got lucky with a wall jump and damage-boost, but I can honestly imagine most people wont be able to beat this boss, which sucks

Conclusion:
I like most of Newer Super Luigi Wii Dark Moon, but some aspects are way to annoyingly difficult
