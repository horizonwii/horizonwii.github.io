Rules for creating a project thread

You must follow these in order to keep up a project thread.



Tags
    Your thread must be accompained with the correct tag:
  • Ongoing - Hack is currently being worked on
  • Released - Hack is released
  • Hiatus - Hack is on hold
  • Dead - Hack is cancled


  • Projects ongoing for 5 months or more with no progress updates will automatically be placed onto 'hiatus'.
  • Projects on hiatus for 3 months will be automatically considered 'dead'.
  • To revive a dead hack, contact a staff member with more pieces of work completed.
  • If your hack dies, you will also be banned from making another project thread for a certain amount of time (depending on how many dead projected you've made).


Requirments
  • You must show at LEAST 5 screen shots of your hack
    - Don't make them all the same level.
    - If you have a video, say a trailer, you only need 3 screenshots.
    - Logo's do NOT COUNT as an image.
  • Hack must be accompanied with a good description.
  • Levels must have effort put into them.
  • All original levels. If anything is taken from the depo your thread will be trashed for plagiarism.

Things to remember
  • Do you really have the dedication to make a hack?
    - Making a hack, even if it's a small one, is very time consuming and will require a lot of effort.
    - If you want a team then be prepared to make sure everyone is motivated and dedicated, along with making sure
    everything is in tip top condition. It's up to you to do the most work to keep production flowing.
  • Do you really want to make your own hack?
    - There are probably some hacks out there that you can join, rather than making your own. Making your own hack is tedious, and a bad step for newcomers.
    - Many people who are motivated to make something will often turn to making their own hack. The right thing to do is to join a hack and dedicate yourself along with others to make something nice.
  • Are you a good leader?
    - Regardless if you have a team or going solo, being a good director/leader is crucial. If you know you quit easily, lose interest regularly, are busy most of the time, or even just lazy, join a hack. This way, whatever you make will contribute to that hack even if you leave. If you trash a custom made hack, chances are, it'll stay trashed.
  • Please don't set unrealistic goals.
    - Right out the gate: If you do not have any sort of coding knowledge, please don't put "custom sprites!" in your hack description. Unless you have a team member who can do this, you'll end up disappointing a lot of people including yourself.
    - Stick with something you know how to do. A small hack with levels you legitimately put effort in is better than a flashy hack with false promises.

    Threads that do not comply with these rules will be trashed within 24 hours.

Posted by Danster
If you want to make the bgB background a single image, use this:
https://mega.nz/#!WdZmlAgQ!xhGSSQqwoU15VpFkAZvv9HnA7MdyCdz3aYxlPKNJVaY

Or, you could use an existing BG and texture edit it.

You can try replacing the models of other backgrounds, but I'm not exactly sure how.

This tutorial will focus on giving your background its own unique ID/slot.
You first need to name your background. For example, bgA_1111 for a Scenery
bg. Or, bgB_2354 for a backdrop bg. It doesn't matter too much. It just needs
to correspond with the 4 # pattern.

You also want to make sure the bgA or bgB makes sense. Meaning, it's bgA if it's a Scenery bg, and
it's bgB if it's a Backdrop bg. Name the file with your new ID, but don't forget the original one.

After you have all that sorted out,

open the background's arc file in a Hex Editor. I
suggest using HxD.

Now, what you need to do is replace the original file name of the BG with your new ID.
The original file name doesn't only appear once. It will appear multiple times at
different parts of the arc file.

-There are 2 ways of doing this. You could scope through the Hex Editor and replace each
time the name shows.

-Or, you could use HxD's "Replace" option and do it that way.

open the arc file in BrawlBox. Any version of it is fine.

Change each time the original file name appears to your new file name. It should appear about 2 or 3 times.

-The brres name
-the model name
-the animation texture name

It may appear at other places, but I'm not sure.

Once you've finished that, save and close the arc file. That's it! You're done!

 The level depo is a place to share your levels to the public easily.

If you're unfamiliar with it, then let me tell fill you in.


The depo can be sorted by theme, size, and even game. When creating your upload, you must insert in those fields.


The list of themes is very expensive, so check that out in due time. Sizes, however, include small, medium, large, and extra large. Games include NSMBW, NewerSMBW, and NSMBU.

After giving your level a nice description, a theme, size, and game, it's time to actually upload your level. To do this, visit Mediafire or Dropbox, upload, and get the link. Now put that link into the link field and you're good.


Once you've posted, your level is out into the public! Here's an example of how that might look:


From here, users can rate your level by either liking/disliking your level. As you can see, this level has 3 likes and 0 dislikes.


You can also search through uploads using a specific theme, length, and game or flip through the pages of the depo.



Coming soon is a following/replies feature. When those are added this post will be updated.

Other than that, that's about it for the depo. Make sure to check it out!
